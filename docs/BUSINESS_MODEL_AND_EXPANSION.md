# BaseballAI Coach — Business Model & Expansion Plan

## 1. Product Positioning

BaseballAI Coach is a private AI-powered youth baseball performance analysis web app.

The first product focus is pitching analysis for young athletes. A parent, coach, or trainer records a pitching video, uploads it to the web app, and receives mechanical feedback, progress tracking, and simple coaching recommendations.

The long-term vision is to become an AI sports development platform for youth athletes, teams, coaches, and training facilities.

## 2. Core Value Proposition

### For Parents

Parents want to help their child improve, but they often do not have access to professional coaching after every practice or game.

BaseballAI Coach gives parents:
- Simple video-based pitching feedback
- Progress tracking over time
- Clear improvement notes
- A private record of their child's development
- A way to prepare better for lessons, practice, and games

### For Youth Coaches

Youth coaches manage many players and cannot deeply analyze every pitch, swing, or movement manually.

BaseballAI Coach gives coaches:
- Player video libraries
- AI-generated mechanics notes
- Session comparison
- Team-level progress tracking
- Objective feedback to support coaching conversations

### For Training Facilities

Baseball academies and private instructors need differentiation, retention, and scalable coaching tools.

BaseballAI Coach gives facilities:
- White-label athlete dashboards
- Coach workflow tools
- Session reports
- Player progress history
- A premium technology feature they can offer families

## 3. Initial Target Customers

## 10. Go-To-Market Plan

## Phase 1: Family MVP Validation

Goal:
Prove parents want simple AI video feedback.

Actions:
- Test with your grandson
- Test with 5–10 parents
- Use phone-recorded pitching videos
- Collect before/after feedback
- Keep product private and simple

Success signals:
- Parents understand the report
- Parents upload more than one video
- Parents ask for progress comparison
- Coaches find the notes useful
- Users are willing to pay $10–20/month

## Phase 2: Local Coach Pilot

Goal:
Validate coach workflow.

Actions:
- Offer free pilot to 2–3 local baseball coaches
- Let each coach test with 5–10 players
- Add coach notes
- Add PDF/export report
- Improve language of feedback

Success signals:
- Coach saves time
- Coach uses dashboard during lessons
- Parents like the reports
- Coach asks for team/facility pricing

## Phase 3: Training Facility Pilot

Goal:
Validate B2B pricing.

Actions:
- Approach local baseball academies
- Offer branded portal pilot
- Add facility dashboard
- Add monthly player progress report
- Charge pilot fee if possible

Success signals:
- Facility sees it as a premium service
- Facility wants branding
- Facility wants multiple coach seats
- Facility uses reports for retention

## 11. Competitive Positioning

BaseballAI Coach should not position itself as replacing human coaches.

It should position itself as:

"AI video analysis that helps parents, coaches, and young players see progress more clearly."

Avoid:
- "Replaces coaches"
- "Prevents injury"
- "Medical-grade"
- "Guaranteed performance improvement"

Use:
- "Support tool"
- "Mechanics feedback"
- "Progress tracking"
- "Private athlete development record"
- "Coach-friendly analysis"

## 12. Product Differentiation

BaseballAI Coach can differentiate through:

- Youth-first design
- Parent-friendly language
- Private video storage
- Simple reports
- Longitudinal progress tracking
- Coach collaboration
- Affordable pricing
- No expensive hardware
- Phone-first video capture

## 13. MVP Scope Guard

The MVP should not include:

- Native mobile app
- Live camera streaming
- Real-time edge AI
- Marketplace
- Payments
- Team billing
- Custom trained ML model
- Face recognition
- Public video sharing
- Medical injury diagnosis
- Recruiting platform
- Professional scouting claims

The MVP should include:

- Login
- Athlete profile
- Pitching video upload
- Pose extraction
- Basic metrics
- AI-generated feedback
- Session history
- Progress dashboard
- Video delete function

## 15. Safety, Privacy, and Child Protection Rules

Because the product involves minors, safety must be part of the business model.

Required rules:

- Parent or guardian account required
- Private video storage by default
- No public profiles
- No public sharing
- Clear delete controls
- No sale of child video data
- No face recognition in MVP
- No targeted advertising to children
- Strong consent language
- Clear disclaimers that feedback is educational, not medical advice

## 18. Recommended Next Business Step

Do not start with payments or a large commercial launch.

Start with a private beta:

- Your grandson
- 3–5 other players
- 1 local coach
- 10–20 pitching videos

The goal is to validate:

- Can the system process normal phone videos?
- Are the reports useful?
- Do parents understand the feedback?
- Do coaches trust the output?
- What would someone pay for this?

After this validation, add subscription plans and coach/team features.
